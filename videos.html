Video 3 - EMA vs SMA

This video explains a traders uses moving averages to monitor stock price trends through time. A Simple Moving Average computes the average price for a specified period and has a lower reaction speed to change than an Exponential Moving Average (EMA), which provides a greater weight to recent prices, and has a faster reaction speed. Both SMAs and EMAs are similarly used by traders in identifying trends, areas of support or resistance, and potential buy or sell signals.
